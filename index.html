01 · What they are

The three families of words

Each family has one job. Learn the job and you will always know which word to use.

Personal pronouns

pronombres personales (sujeto)

They replace the name of the person or thing that does the action. They go before the verb.

I · you · he · she · it · we · they
JOB → be the subject of the sentence

Possessive adjectives

adjetivos posesivos

They show who owns something and always sit before a noun (my book, your phone).

my · your · his · her · its · our · their
JOB → describe a noun (always + noun)

Possessive pronouns

pronombres posesivos

They also show ownership, but they stand alone and replace the noun (It's mine).

mine · yours · his · hers · ours · theirs
JOB → replace the noun (no noun after)
02 · The confusing part

Adjective or pronoun?

This is where most people slip. The trick is simple: look at what comes after the word.

Adjective needs a noun

A possessive adjective is never alone — a noun follows it.

This is my phone.
I like her idea.
Their house is big.

Pronoun stands alone

A possessive pronoun replaces the noun — nothing follows it.

This phone is mine.
The idea was hers.
That big house is theirs.

Watch out: its vs it's

its = possessive (the dog and its bone). it's = "it is". They sound the same but mean different things.

Good news: his never changes

For "he", the adjective and the pronoun are the same word: his car · the car is his. One word, two jobs.
